Audience Experience of New Media Dance Performances

Introduction

We would like to invite you to be part of this research project, if you would like to. You should only agree to take part if you want to, it is entirely up to you. If you choose not to take part there won’t be any disadvantages for you and you will hear no more about it.
Please read the following information carefully before you decide to take part; this will tell you why the research is being done and what you will be asked to do if you take part.
If you decide to take part, on the next page you will be asked to confirm your consent.
You are still free to withdraw at any time and without giving a reason by simply leaving the study.

We are conducting this research as part of the postgraduate course "Media and Arts Technology" at Queen Mary University London.


Instructions

This study aims to find out more about the audience experience of dance performances that include new media. You will be presented with a short video of a recorded performance. You will only be able to watch this performance once, so please make sure that you are in a calm environment where you can focus on both video and sound. It might be best to use headphones. During the video you will be asked to indicate which parts of the performance you like or dislike by clicking the respective button. After you have watched the video, you will be asked to answer a few questions about the performance. This study should take about 5 to 10 minutes and it is advised to complete it on a desktop computer or laptop. The survey might not function properly on a mobile device.

It is up to you to decide whether or not to take part. If you do decide to take part please click “Next”. You will be asked to confirm your consent on the next page.

If you have any questions or concerns about the manner in which the study was conducted please, in the first instance, contact the researcher responsible for the study Sebastian Löbbers - s.lobbers@qmul.ac.uk

Video and real-time assessment

This is the first part of the study. You will be presented with one 2:40 min long video of a dance performance. While watching the video please indicate that you find a specific moment especially good or bad by clicking the respective button under the video (see image below). After clicking a button a marker will appear under the video. The green button with a thumb-up indicates a good moment and the red button with a thumb-down a bad one. A good moment could show a pleasing movement, great synchronicity between dance, music and/or visuals or anything else with a positive connotation. A bad moment could show a disjunction between dance, music and/or visuals, a perceived error or anyhting else with a negative connotation. Please do not focus solely on the choreography, music or technical setup but rather indicate your perception of the system as a whole.
The video will start automatically once you press "Start" and cannot be stopped or rewound, so please pay attention. Once the video stopped playing you will automatically be redirected to the next stage of the study.
